The Penalty Box

Does aggressive play pay off?

Posted by OhSnapp on May 5, 2017

Hockey is an aggressive sport. The average fan walks away from a game unsatisfied if they don't get to watch at least one fight break out. Power Plays make hockey the exciting game it is. But in the interest of winning a game, just how valuable is it to be an aggressive player?

The average player ends up in the penalty box for just under a minute per game. Dave "Tiger" Williams, on the other hand, maintained an average of 4.2 minutes per game, amassing a whopping 3.07 hours of penalty time over his career. Considering he made over a million dollars at the peak of his career, that's a lot of money for the Maple Leafs to shell out on their penalty box.

In general, there seems to be an inverse relationship between penalty minutes and PPG. The logic feels right--hopping in and out of the box can interrupt a player's rhythm while well-behaved players remain focused on scoring points. That's certainly the case for Wayne Gretzky, the all-time leader in PPG who averaged only 0.4 penalty minutes (that's 24 seconds!) per game.
